Our Vision

The American University of Iraq-Baghdad is a world-class institution of higher learning established in Baghdad that admitted its first students on February 14, 2021.

Our mission is to help rebuild Iraq’s educational and cultural legacy and to serve the people of Iraq, the Middle East, and the global community. Accredited in Iraq by the Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research, and equipped with a rigorous academic curriculum and a diverse team of distinguished international faculty, the university has built its educational practices and standards on the philosophies and values of the American liberal arts model of higher education.

AUIB strives to build a supportive, nurturing, intellectual environment on a foundation of cultural and religious tolerance and the free exchange of ideas where education, research and community service generate well-rounded global citizens, leaders, and innovators, who are equipped with such values as integrity, critical thinking, and a deep appreciation for lifelong learning.

Our Mission

AUIB distinguishes itself as a unique and diverse learning community that offers the highest caliber of education.

AUIB strives to provide continuous innovation in teaching and research to prepare its learners to become principled and productive community leaders in today’s ever-changing societies.

AUIB offers a positive, transformative, cultural, and intellectual environment that is respectful of diversity and dialogue and that will equip graduates with the leadership skills needed to serve the people of Iraq and address the challenges of the region and the world.

Our Pledge

An AUIB education builds a strong liberal arts foundation, adding breadth of knowledge to students’ depth of academic specialization. As such, required courses will include a wide array of subjects that build students’ scientific tools and practical skills, and also broaden their cultural horizons.  
The majority of time during the first two years of study (freshman and sophomore years) will be spent studying various courses in the liberal arts, such as psychology, sociology, history, English literature, archaeology, biology, etc. Students will not begin to specialize in a specific discipline until after they have successfully completed a major part of the Core Liberal Arts (CLA) program. 

The focus is on critical thinking, not rote memorization. The goal is to encourage students to become well educated in a wide variety of subjects and to become life-long learners. In other words, an AUIB education will focus on depth and breadth of coursework outside of a specific discipline. 

AUIB recruits highly diverse faculty members who hold terminal degrees (mostly PhDs) from North American and European universities but come from around the world. This diversity helps provide students with a global perspective on all topics. AUIB faculty members will go out of their way to help students develop a well-rounded and balanced character, intellectually, psychologically, socially, and physically. 

An English-language curriculum is the heart and soul of a true American-style education. Thus, at AUIB, courses are taught in English, with an emphasis on developing strong writing, reading, and speaking skills that will advance a graduate’s career. This means:
  A superior English Language Academy 

  • English-language Instruction 
  • English-language textbooks 
  • Native English-speaking faculty, with at least one degree from an American or other Western university in which the language of instruction was English. 

AUIB is on track to begin institutional and programmatic accreditation of the university itself and all its colleges, as appropriate. Although this takes time (sometimes 7-10 years after opening), there was determination right from the outset to gain accreditation, so all its programs, policies, and procedures are being set up to the high standards required for U.S. accreditation.

Partnerships with other international universities is a key aspect of any true American-style university. At AUIB, study abroad programs that are continually established allow students to gain knowledge of outside cultures, religions, politics and more. Studying in a foreign country inspires independent thinking, self-confidence, and the ability to see the world from a different perspective.

At any true American-style university, admission requirements are far different than at national universities, where acceptance is based solely on the results of a comprehensive exam taken at the last year of high school.

At AUIB, the Admissions Committee considers for admission the “whole person”, and thus evaluates not only a student’s grades, but also his/her personality, statement of purpose, involvement in professional and/or extracurricular activities, letters of recommendation, and other factors.  

At AUIB, academic freedom for all faculty is a given. Faculty will be involved in the governance of the institution and in shaping the institutional vision and mission statements. 
As in most American-style universities, AUIB will develop a tenure track for all full-time faculty. 

AUIB will have a low student to faculty ratio, generally 15:1 and certainly not more than 20:1, except in a very few freshman-level courses. In addition, AUIB faculty will be active researchers and will involve students in their research activities, which is also a key part of a student’s education. 

AUIB guarantees strong student support services, including advising, counseling, athletics, computer labs, research facilities, extra-curricular activities, and more.
Any true America-style university will be deeply involved in the community by encouraging both faculty and students to develop a wide variety of programs that benefit those around them. AUIB develops close ties with local and regional communities, and engages them via a wide array of programs, initiatives, and activities.
