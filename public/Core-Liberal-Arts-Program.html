Core Liberal Arts Program

Liberal arts education fosters the creation of well-rounded students exposed to various academic areas of interest. In addition, it offers them a base of general knowledge that helps them excel in their specialized major fields of inquiry.

Setting AUIB apart from most colleges and universities in the region, AUIB offers a program of Core Liberal Arts (CLA) to provide students with a breadth of multi-disciplinary knowledge. Through CLA’s focused, multi-disciplinary exploration of the liberal arts, students learn to use their education actively to consider new and different perspectives continually as they define their own identities and participate in building thoughtful, engaged communities within the University and beyond. The CLA provides undergraduate students with a quality education that expands their understanding of the world around them and awakens their desire for life-long learning and pursuit of excellence.

The CLA aims to create generations of well-rounded youth versed in the Humanities, Social Sciences, Mathematics, and Natural Sciences. AUIB’s CLA focuses on communication and decision-making skills and analytical, critical, and ethical thinking to prepare students for professional careers in the public, private or nonprofit sectors. Through CLA’s focused, multidisciplinary exploration of the liberal arts, students can become active participants in their education by defining their identities and participating in their communities, University, and beyond.

Students admitted to AUIB are expected to complete 42 credits from various fields. Core liberal arts courses comprise courses from English communication skills, humanities, social sciences, life sciences, and quantitative reasoning fields, in addition to the first-year university experience:

English communication skills

Expository Writing, Academic Writing

Humanities

Social Sciences

Natural Sciences

Quantitative Reasoning

The Core Liberal Arts Program curriculum is guided by the following principles and student learning outcomes (SLOs)

Considering new and different perspectives as they define their own identities and participate in building thoughtful, engaged communities within the University and beyond (Self and World).
Analyzing information from multiple perspectives and being able to develop reasoned arguments and/or solutions to issues within the subject areas being examined (Critical Thinking)
Engaging in scientific/mathematical inquiry to examine and develop academic capacity to participate in scientific and quantitative observation and reasoning (Qualitative Literacy).
Developing the capacity to communicate knowledge, ideas, and arguments clearly and effectively in written and oral forms, and appropriate to studied disciplines (Communication).
Finding, evaluating, and effectively synthesizing appropriate sources in conveying an idea or making an argument (Information Literacy).
