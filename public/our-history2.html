Our History

  • The American University of Iraq-Baghdad (AUIB) is a nonprofit institution of higher learning that was founded in Baghdad in 2018 and opened its doors to its first class of students on February 14, 2021. The motivation behind the establishment of AUIB began from a vision to reestablish Iraq, and particularly Baghdad, as the heart of higher learning, and as the capital of education and culture in the Middle East, especially as Baghdad was all but destroyed in the face of continuous strife and wars over the past several decades. 

  • Inspired by Iraq’s historic role as the cradle of civilization, AUIB follows in the tradition of three historical institutions in Baghdad: The House of Wisdom, Al-Nizamiyah Madrasah, and Al- Mustansiriyah Madrasah. The first of these, the House of Wisdom, a precursor to modern universities, was a major intellectual center from the 9th to the 13th centuries. For nearly 500 years, this center of teaching and humanities hosted intellectual elites from all over the world and was the largest repository of books. The second of these great institutions, al-Nizamiyah Madrasah of Baghdad, was established in 1065, and offered free education. Within thirty years, it housed over 3000 students and was described as the “largest university in the medieval world.” Finally, the al-Mustansiriyah Madrasah was established in 1227 and taught subjects such as medicine, math, literature, grammar, philosophy and Islamic religious studies and Islamic law.

  • All three remarkable educational institutions were ransacked and destroyed during the Mongol invasions in the 13th century. Despite these challenges, Baghdad remained the intellectual and cultural capital of the Arab world and remained a vibrant and thriving city that enjoyed freedom of expression and cultural and religious tolerance. Inspired by the continuing legacy of these three historical educational institutions and by the desire to restore Iraq to its former glory, the founders of AUIB, Saadi Saihood and his three sons, focused on founding a university that will lead the way to the complete revival of Iraq through education and higher learning, based on the core values of liberal arts education.
