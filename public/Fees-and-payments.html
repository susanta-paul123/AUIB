Solidarity Scholarship

With the pandemic and its effects on the global economy, supply chains, international travel, and global energy demands, Iraq and its people have suffered financially for reasons beyond their control. The American University of Iraq-Baghdad (AUIB) is in total solidarity with all Iraqis in overcoming these financial challenges.

To help citizens get through these difficult times and yet still allow them to pursue their dreams of high-quality university education, AUIB is pleased to announce the "Solidarity Scholarship" that will be awarded to every student entering AUIB in Spring 2022. For the next incoming class of students, every student will get a 30% reduction on their tuition. We will lock in that rate until they finish their degrees while maintaining good academic standing.

AUIB is a university of opportunity for the youth of Iraq. We stand with you and your families during these challenging times. We are committed to your success and stand ready to help you reach your goals, fulfill your dreams, and be successful in life.

Payments

Payments for courses and fees will be made in the Enrollment Management (Building 36). Students can make payments in cash (U.S. Dollars and Iraqi Dinars). You will need to contact finance@auib.edu.iq to obtain information and instructions.

Payment Installment Plan

The monthly payment plan is available for those who are unable to pay the full tuition at the beginning of the semester, by making a Monthly Payment Agreement with the University. You will need to either visit the Finance Office (Building 52) to make these payment arrangements or contact us via email finance@auib.edu.iq for further instructions.

Repeating Courses

Repeating Courses Policy

Students who complete a course with a low or failing grade may wish to repeat the course in a subsequent semester. In addition to understanding the policies concerning course repeats (see below) and speaking with an academic advisor, it is also helpful for a student to consider the following:

  • If you wish to increase your GPA, consider whether this is the best way to achieve that goal. Most students will find that when they repeat a course, there is only a small impact on their overall GPA. Another way to increase your GPA is to take additional courses and do well in them. Finally, suppose a student is repeating a course to improve their GPA for their graduate or professional school applications. In that case, it is helpful to know that these schools may calculate a student's GPA differently when repeat courses appear on the transcript.
  • Payment terms for students who repeat a course are as below:

Late fees Policy

Late payment fees are assessed once per term as follows:
