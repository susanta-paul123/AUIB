Community Parternships

AUIB’s mission to serve the community, and its grand objective to contribute substantially to the revival of Iraq, materializes in part in the social impact of its developing programs, partnerships, and activities. AUIB engages different institutions and community organizations that are important actors in humanitarian intervention, in the education and health sectors, in social service, and in formulating socioeconomic public policies that directly impact the lives of citizens.  

Over the past year alone, and to give but a few examples of its community engagement initiatives in various fields, AUIB has established a collaborative partnership with the Iraqi Red Crescent society, whereby the University provides services to the humanitarian organization, chief of which is English language instruction to Red Crescent members and volunteers. And by the initiative of the students of AUIB’s College of Pharmacy, and in collaboration with the Iraqi National Blood Transfusion Center, AUIB organized its first blood donation campaign on campus. Also, in the field of humanitarian support and health, AUIB’s Student Activities Board, in collaboration with the Student Scientific Society of AUIB’s College of Pharmacy, organized a Breast Cancer Awareness Campaign, during which a seminar was held on the topic of cancer prevention, diagnosis, and treatment. The University also hosted several training workshops in the medical sphere, such as on Therapeutic Percutaneous Coronary Intervention and on Hysteroscopy.  

In the field of education, AUIB and Vanderbilt University’s Peabody College of Education and Human Development launched a partnership initiative, financed by a 2.5 million USD grant from the US Department of State, dedicated to “developing a unique and powerful program to revolutionize K-12 (Kindergarten through 12th grade) education in Iraq,” by means of building and supporting model schools throughout the country. This will be done primarily through the establishment of the AUIB College of Education for training school teachers and principals, as well as conducting research on education in Iraq to help policy makers in reforming all levels of education in Iraq. 

AUIB also continues to forge partnerships with government institutions and community associations that will contribute the process of recovery and rehabilitation that Iraqi society is undergoing, following the years of bloodshed and destruction. In this regard, AUIB and the Ministry of Displacement and Migration have recently outlined their collaborative partnership, whereby the University would contribute studies and programs on the themes of “recovery, psychological rehabilitation, and the (re-)integration” of the displaced, and also on the subject of “diversity, minorities, and peace,” by the words of Minister Evan Faiek Jabru. In the same context, AUIB has partnered with the “Martyrs and Political Prisoners Foundation and the Ministry of Health to help identify the remains of missing persons, thus bringing some peace to their families, by allowing them a proper burial of the remains of their loved ones, and contributing to the national healing and reconciliation process.  

AUIB is also keen to engage non-profit community organizations dedicated to social service, providing them support in various forms, as in offering English language scholarships to the students of associations such as the “Al-Ghad Al-Mushreq Foundation for Orphans Care.”  
In the past year, in the realm of socioeconomic public policy, AUIB’s Center for Policy Research has co-organized, in partnership with the International Labour Organization, the “National Conference on Social Security and Public Works in Iraq.” The conference discussed “developing national policies for the creation of decent work opportunities” for all, which would in turn sustain the expansion of the social security system to include all residents of Iraq.  

Iraq’s rich and unique cultural heritage is also among AUIB’s concerns, as the University has also collaborated with initiatives of parties such as Iraq’s State Board of Antiquities and Heritage and the World Monuments Fund, aiming to preserve and develop Iraq’s most prominent archaeological sites.
