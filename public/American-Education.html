American Education

An American-style education allows not only for instruction in the English language, but also enables students to be active and intentional participants in their own education through a culture and ethos of higher education that is validated through U.S. accreditation. This is what AUIB offers the people of Iraq: a strict adherence to the standards of the best Universities in the United States, an all-encompassing liberal arts higher education, the promotion of critical thinking, effective communication, cultural awareness, and a commitment to lifelong learning. 

American universities are not necessarily affiliated with each other and are not governed by a single body or association. Each institution has its own curriculum, mission, and value statements. Institutional accreditation provides the overarching quality control mechanism that ensures consistency in programs and services. However, there are four essential areas that these universities must adhere to. 
These include the liberal arts tradition, student life, governance of the institution, and teaching standards:

Liberal Arts tradition: 

Many so-called “American-style universities” outside the United States do not follow the liberal arts tradition common to universities in the U.S. What distinguishes a true American-style university from others is its broad, liberal arts curriculum. An American university’s liberal nature is based on developing critical thinking skills, oral and written communication, and emphasizing lifelong learning. In a true American-style university, students are not merely prepared for a job after college but are prepared for a lifetime of active learning, able to adapt to the changing requirements of the economy and workforce.

Student Life: 

An American-style university provides students with many fruitful opportunities for growth, including opportunities for Study Abroad and internships, but most especially engagement in extracurricular activities, including student athletics, clubs, and organizations, in addition to counseling, health care, and student support services.

Faculty Governance and Involvement:  

In true American-style universities, Faculty are empowered to do much more than teach and maintain high morale. Faculty are directly involved in the governance of the university, as is required by accrediting agencies in the U.S. By including faculty in the university’s decision-making processes, American-style universities retain quality professors, thereby strengthening the student experience by decreasing faculty turnover.

Teaching Standards:  

A strong focus on teaching is critical to the success of a university. In American-style universities, professors typically teach only two or three courses with time outside the classroom for research and individual student consultations. Instead of carrying a four- or even five-course teaching load per semester, faculty can focus on their research, and publish high-quality, peer-reviewed research and help students through one-on-one meetings to help them understand classroom materials better. With attention on research, faculty can stay up-to-date on their subject matter and students can gain valuable insights into research methodology. In addition, student/faculty ratios should remain low; at AUIB, the preferred class size averages 15:1 (students: faculty), and 20:1 in certain freshmen introductory courses. This practice ensures that students can participate in classroom discussions better and have a stronger relationship with their professors.
