Center for Policy Research

The Center for Policy Research (CPR), founded by Dr. Muslih Irwani, Associate Professor of Public and Social Policy, is an independent research center based at the American University of Iraq - Baghdad. It is dedicated to public policy and issues related to Iraq and the Middle East. The CPR works to understand political and socio-economic developments with a national, regional, and international perspective, and develops solutions to public policy challenges to help understand contemporary issues that communities of Iraq and wider Middle East face. It also provides research services, systematic analysis, and innovative thinking to government institutions, non-governmental organizations, foundations, and private-sector firms.

The CPR research themes include, but are not limited to, good governance and sound administration; peace and conflict; ethno-religious issues of Iraq and the wider Middle East; gender; displacement; and violent extremism.

CPR believes in the importance of promoting diversity and integration to achieve Iraq's long-awaited peace and development. However, this peace and development will only be achieved if society subscribes to universal values of peace and democratic governance. CPR hopes to contribute to this project through independent research that promotes freedom of thought, expression, and liberal values.

Objectives

CPR aims to provide policy-oriented solutions to Iraq's social, economic, and political issues. Our objectives are:

  • Establish an evidence-based research hub and think tank in Iraq.
  • Host researchers interested in Iraq and the Middle East.
  • Establish AUIB as a source of research, information and high-quality analyses for government, academic community, NGOs (local and international), and think tanks.
  • To become a link between issues and policymakers, practitioners, and stake holders.
  • To contribute to the capacity building in the public sector (local, regional, and national) in the practice of public policy.
