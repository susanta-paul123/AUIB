Why Study at AUIB’s CAS?

AUIB’s College of Arts & Sciences (CAS) offers degree programs that combine breadth and depth of knowledge, by building students’ general knowledge, as well as educating and training them in specialized fields of Humanities, Social Sciences, Natural Sciences, and Applied Sciences. Students learn and employ a range of approaches in examining ideas and phenomena, historical and contemporary events, and life’s eternal and ever-evolving questions.

Graduates’ Prospective Careers

CAS programs qualify graduates to work in broad and diverse fields. Graduates may choose to pursue careers in the public or private sectors, in academia, research and development, administration, services, and a wide array of professions and industries.

Depending on the program from which a student graduates, (s)he may work in bioindustries, pharmaceutical industries, chemical engineering, food industries, the energy sector, petrochemical industries, environmental research and consulting, various governmental bodies and regulatory agencies, automotive and aerospace industries, electronics and robotics, manufacturing & production, transportation, telecommunications, programming, information technology systems, information security, or cyber security, as examples.

Graduates of CAS programs may also work in fields such as journalism, film & theater, publishing, marketing, public relations, interpretation/translation, cultural resource management, psychotherapy, social service, human resources management, foreign service, education, and public service.

CoB Values

The foundational pillar of CAS values is Integrity in teaching, learning, conducting research, and engaging the broader community. Scientific Curiosity, Innovation, and Life-Long Learning are the driving forces behind the creation of knowledge through discovery

and the formulation of practical solutions to real-life problems. CAS fosters a culture of free intellectual exchange in a respectful, encouraging environment, thus creating a healthy setting for innovation and intellectual development.

CAS Programs

Depending on their course of study, students who successfully complete a CAS Program will graduate with either a Bachelor of Arts (BA) or a Bachelor of Science (BS) degree: 

BS in Physics

Physics involves studying phenomena from the subnuclear to the scale of the Universe, and from the behavior of a single particle to huge numbers of particles. Physics tries to understand, in a quantitative way, how the world works. The curriculum covers the Mechanical, Electromagnetic, and Quantum aspects of the world. Elective courses usually cover numerous directions in the various subfields of Condensed Matter Physics, High Energy/Nuclear Physics, Astrophysics and Gravitation, and other more specialized topics.

Read More

BS in Biology

Biology is the study of Life, from viral infections to ecological processes like climate change. This study includes understanding all levels of organization: the chemical reactions that power life; how these reactions are organized within cells; how these cells can specialize to form parts of complex organisms; and how these organisms then interact with both members of the same and other species. The curriculum involves laboratory analyses of chemicals, cells, and animals; studying plants and animals in the wild and in agriculture; and increasingly in computing, as we amass huge amounts of information about our genomes.

Read More

BS in Chemistry

Often referred to as the “Central Science,” the study of Chemistry elucidates the properties of matter, the changes they undergo, and the natural laws that describe these changes and how to produce novel and useful new products. Chemistry seeks to understand not only the properties of matter, like the composition and reactivity of elements, but also how and why matter undergoes a variety of changes.  Chemistry is an innovative and imaginative science with many opportunities to make new discoveries, develop new molecules and materials, and increase our understanding of important biological and industrial processes. The program provides dedicated instruction in many branches of chemistry, such as, Analytical, Inorganic, Organic, Physical, Biochemistry, senior research project, and industrial training.

Read More

BS in Computer Science

Computer Science is about problem-solving, by gathering information and communicating with computers using programming languages to create a set of instructions for the computer to solve the problem. This branch of study includes computational theory, computer design, algorithms, and understanding the way we humans interact with technology. The program introduces students to the fundamentals of computer hardware and software, to programming languages, best practices and theory of programming, computer connectivity, computer security, and data organization. A distinct feature of the program is the choice it gives students to pursue different specialization tracks, such as Data Sciences, Artificial and Computational Intelligence, Bioinformatics, Computer Network & Cyber Security, and Mobile application and gaming.

Read More

BA in English Language and Literature

The program sheds light on how Western Europeans and North Americans ponder the questions of life, based on philosophical underpinnings that have their roots in the Ancient Greek and Roman times, and in the Italian Renaissance period. Furthermore, most technology, research and development, and know-how today continue to be developed in the English language. Accordingly, studying the literature that the English and Americans call their own will enable Iraqi graduates to better understand and communicate with these cultures in their professional lives. Simply put, studying English Language and Literature is the gateway to Western culture and global interaction.

Read More

BA in History

History is the study of the sum of human experience. It is concerned with the past and its present legacy. The various strands of the subject include economic, cultural, social, and political history, as well as the history of ideas. All of these topics can be studied at the local, national, or international levels. The study of history develops an array of skills, including independent critical thinking, analytical skills, the ability to process and synthesize today’s vast information flows, and engaging in historiographical debate.

Read More

BA in Archaeology

Archaeology is the scientific study of prehistoric and historic human activities and their physical remains. This includes exploring human origins, early societies, and the transition from hunting and gathering to farming with domesticated crops and livestock. The Archaeology program includes teaching best practices in fieldwork, including excavation and extraction, and trains students in laboratory skills, including the analysis of artifacts using specialized equipment, under controlled conditions. In broader and more fundamental terms, the study of archaeology develops students’ critical thinking, and helps them transcend historical, geographic, cultural, and ethnic barriers.

Read More

BA in Psychology

Psychology is a wide field of study. On the micro level, it examines the human mind and behavior, including therapy and follow-up; while on the macro level, it addresses the interplay between human emotional and mental functions on one hand, and society and culture on the other. This showcases Psychology’s powerful role in understanding both high and popular culture, and the functioning of societies and states. Psychology’s immense usefulness as a broad analytical tool covers the realm of management, business and marketing practices and norms, as well as the realm of medicine, healthcare, and education, as examples.

Read More

Faculty

CoB boasts a team of highly competent and culturally diverse international faculty members who hold highest level degrees from renowned academic institutions in the United States, Europe, and abroad, and combine academic rigor and hands-on practical experience as consultants, managers, bankers, and specialists in fields such as Human Resources Management.

CoB faculty members have been awarded international recognition for their published research, and have engaged in consultancies with prominent international agencies and multinational corporations.
